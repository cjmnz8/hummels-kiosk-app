The Hummel Exhibit

By Library Staff, posted December 30, 2015

The art of Sister Maria Innocentia Hummel began to appear in the 1930s in Germany and Switzerland, often drawings of children. This caught the eye of Franz Goebel who began to turn Hummel's drawing into figures. After WWII, the popularity of Hummel figurines grew as American soldiers stationed in West Germany sent home the figurines as gifts. A vibrant market developed and prices skyrocketed in the 1970s though prices have lowered with production ended in 2008. The figurines were soon resumed under a new organization Hummel Manufaktur GmbH and new ones are released each year.

The display offers early Hummels from the 1930s as well as recent creations from 2014 and 2015.

Barrons Catalog: Guide to buying hummel figurines & hummel collectibles

By Barrons Catalog, posted January 4, 2010

Each M.I. Hummel collectible by Goebel, like the darling Hummel figurines reflecting the joy and innocence of children (Hummel girl figurine)or the angelic purity of a Hummel Madonna, are and have been the most sought after by collectors and gift givers around the world for over 70 years. The Hummel figurine value is observed in the painstaking attention given to each individual piece. Emphasizing originality, excellence and quality over quantity, the creation process of these limited edition figurines include several weeks of over 700 hands-on adjustments, keeping the age-old Goebel tradition of unmatched craftsmanship with each new MI Hummel figurine. Isn't it fascinating how many weeks go into producing one Hummel figurine? Doesn't the vast array of seasons, subjects and situations depicted in a Hummel collectible make it an easy choice when it's time to find a great gift for any occasion? Do you see how these uniquely detailed works of art are an investment that appreciate over the years, while beautifying a room and adding to your collection?

The Hummel figurine began in 1909 when a girl born in a Bavarian village became an artist prodigy. Berta Hummel's father gave up his dreams of an artist for her and provided the financial means for her schooling. She entered boarding school at the Institute of Sisters at the age of 12 after one of the Sisters caught notice of her abilities and recommended her for an accelerated artist training program. It wasn't long until someone noticed her incredible talents. There, Sister Maria Innocentia Hummel added more knowledge to her expertise and gained the attention of a prominent porcelain company in Germany.

W. Goebel Porzellanfabrik, owned by Franz Goebel, happened upon several drawings of Sister Maria Innocentia Hummel and decided to create porcelain figurines depicting Hummel art. When they presented them to her with an offer for mass production the Hummel figurines; she happily accepted and as a true artist, insisted that they leave artist control to the porcelain sculptures and painters. She assisted the painters by helping to match the colors in her drawings to their work. January 9, 1935 is the day she signed her contract and considered the birthday of the MI Hummel figurine. They became instantly adored and are continuously loved for their charming childhood depicitions. The making of Hummel art, whether it be a sweet hummel girl figurine or an innocent Hummel Madonna, all get the same hand made and highly detailed attention from the artists at Hummel to create the preciousness everyone can cherish forever.

It is sad to have lost Sister Hummel at the early age of 37. Her spirit lives through the talent she left for all to enjoy in the porcelain figurines she created or influenced around the world.
